75th Hunger Games

Quarter Quell

"On the seventy-fifth anniversary, as a reminder to the rebels that even the strongest among them cannot overcome the power of the Capitol, the male and female tributes will be reaped from their existing pool of victors."

Arena

Terrain

The pedestals were in a ring around the Cornucopia, which sat on an island in the center of a round salt lake. The sky above them was pink. Rather than offering food and other supplies like usual, the Cornucopia only held weapons this year. Its tail originally pointed to the twelve o'clock sector, but the island could be spun around to disorient tributes. Like a wheel, spokes divided the salt lake into twelve sections, with two tributes in each. These spokes ran from the Cornucopia to the beach. Beyond the beach was a dense jungle, invisibly divided into twelve sections as well. A high-voltage force field enclosed the entire arena in a dome.

Unlike most years, food and drink were not available at the Cornucopia, and there were no tools to facilitate desalinating water from the salt lake. However, the trees were full of fresh water; one could drill a hole into the bark and place a spile to extract the water inside. In terms of food, tributes could forage for edible nuts, or they could hunt some of the jungle's ordinary, if unfamiliar, wildlife. Peeta Mellark discovered that meat could be tossed into the force field to be cooked. Those with sharp weapons like Finnick's trident could spear fish in the salt lake, or they could bring in a shellfish if they fashioned a net; these things could be eaten raw. Fair swimmers could even dive for oysters. Sponsors could also send gifts of food to tributes by parachute like usual

Features

The arena was set up like a clock, with each wedge holding a different threat activated by the hour. The cycle began at midnight on the first night when a bell tolled twelve times, marking the start of the second day. Of the arena's twelve sections, 8 are known:

  • Hour 12-1: Lightning bolts struck a specific tree.
  • Hour 1-2: Thick, hot, blood rained down on tributes.
  • Hour 2-3: Poisonous fog causing painful blistering, paralysis, and death.
  • Hour 3-4: Vicious monkey mutts with orange fur, fangs, and claws that came out like switchblades attacked the tributes.
  • Hour 4-5: Jabberjays that tortured tributes with the screams of their loved ones.
  • Hour 5-6: Unknown.
  • Hour 6-7: A giant mutation known as "The Beast" that tore it's victims apart.
  • Hour 7-8: Unknown.
  • Hour 8-9: Unknown.
  • Hour 9-10: Unknown.
  • Hour 10-11: A giant wave swept across the entire sector.
  • Hour 11-12: Noisy insects with pincers that can strip tributes to the bone in seconds.
