74th Hunger Games

Arena

Terrain

The tributes were launched on their pedestals in a ring around the Cornucopia, which sat in the center of an open plain of hard packed dirt. On one side of the cornocopia there was a steep downward slope into a field of tall grass and grain. On the other side and to the right was a lake, and to the left was a forest, sloping downwards further in. Even deeper into that forest, there was a larger variety of trees, a pond, some isolated pools of water, and a marshy area. There was also a copse, concealed by low, dense bushes, where can surveil the lake unseen. In the forest was a long stream, eventually leading into rocky terrain eith muddy bank. The lake sits in the north, and a river curves around from the arena's eastern edge, encompassed by a forest of evergreens.

There was a great deal of ordinary wildlife that tributes could hunt or ensnare for meat, if they had the right skills and weapons or tools. Tributes could also forage for edible plants. The field of grain could be used as a source of food, and there were many berry bushes in the forest; some were edible, but some held poisonous nightlock berries. For water, tributes could turn to the lake, the arena's one major stream, a pond, or isolated pools of water; however, in the interest of safety, these sources had to be disinfected with iodine before drinking.

Features

  • Tracker Jackers - wasp mutts that tracked victims far from their nests, and their venom was highly hallucinogenic, proving fatal in high concentrations.
  • Mockingjays - Harmless songbirds that could repeat tunes.
  • Wolf mutts made to represent each of the dead tributes. Their eyes were remarkably human. Each wolf mutt wore a collar with the number of the tribute's district. They could balance on their hind legs, jump very high, and had four-inch, razor-sharp claws. The mutts were also able to gesture to one another like humans would.
